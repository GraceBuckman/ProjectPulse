Marble Run

Supplies

  • Cardboard tubes (paper towel or toilet paper rolls)
  • Cardboard sheets
  • Scissors
  • Tape or glue
  • Marbles or small balls

Instructions

Design the Track: Plan your marble run layout using cardboard tubes and sheets. Cut and Assemble: Cut the cardboard tubes into various lengths to create ramps and chutes. Secure them to a cardboard base using tape or glue, arranging them to form a track.

Test the Run

Place marbles at the start of the track and observe their path through the different sections.

What it Teaches

  • Physics of Motion: Understand how gravity and momentum affect the movement of objects.
  • Engineering Design: Explore concepts of track design and how to manage flow and obstacles.

Rubber Band-Powered Helicopter

Supplies

  • Wooden craft sticks (popsicle sticks)
  • Rubber bands
  • Paper or plastic for blades
  • Glue
  • Scissors

Instructions

Create the Rotor Blades: Cut paper or plastic into blade shapes and attach them to the ends of two craft sticks to form a rotor.

Assemble the Helicopter: Glue another craft stick to the center of the rotor blades, creating the main body of the helicopter.
Add Rubber Bands: Secure a rubber band to the rotor and twist it to create tension.

Test the Helicopter

Release the rotor to see if it spins and flies.

What it Teaches

  • Aerodynamics: Learn about the principles of lift and rotation.
  • Mechanical Energy: Understand how stored energy in the rubber band can be converted into rotational motion.

Floating Egg

Supplies

DIY Parachute

Supplies

  • Plastic bag or lightweight fabric
  • String or thread
  • Scissors
  • Tape
  • Small toy or weight

Instructions

Create the Parachute: Cut the plastic bag or fabric into a large square. Tape strings evenly around the edges of the square.

Attach the Weight: Tie the ends of the strings to a small toy or weight.
Test the Parachute: Drop the parachute from a height and observe how it slows the descent of the weight.

What it Teaches

  • Air Resistance: Learn about how parachutes use air resistance to slow down objects.
  • Forces and Motion: Understand the relationship between gravity and drag forces.

Simple Gear System

Supplies

  • Cardboard
  • Plastic or metal gears (can be purchased or made)
  • Scissors
  • Glue or tape
  • Small motor (optional)

Instructions

Create Gear Plates: Cut cardboard into circles and attach plastic or metal gears to them using glue or tape.

Assemble the Gears: Arrange and connect the gear plates to create a gear system. Ensure that the gears mesh correctly.
Test the System: Rotate one gear and observe how it turns the connected gears. Optionally, attach a small motor to see how it affects the system.

What it Teaches

  • Gear Mechanics: Understand how gears transmit motion and torque.
  • Mechanical Advantage: Learn about how gear ratios affect speed and force in a mechanical system.

Homemade Hydraulic Lift

Supplies

  • Two syringes (10 ml)
  • Plastic tubing
  • Water
  • Cardboard
  • Hot glue gun
  • Scissors
  • Tape

Instructions

Prepare Syringes: Fill both syringes with water and connect them with the plastic tubing. Make sure there are no air bubbles in the tubing.

Create Lift Platform: Cut out a piece of cardboard to serve as the platform for the lift. Attach it to one of the syringes using hot glue.
Assemble the Lift: Attach the other syringe to a base (also made of cardboard) and glue the other end of the platform to this syringe.
Test the Lift: Push the plunger of one syringe to raise the platform. You should see the platform lift as you push.

What it Teaches

  • Hydraulics: This project demonstrates the principles of hydraulic systems, where fluid pressure is used to perform mechanical work.
  • Simple Machines: It shows how forces can be magnified and transferred using syringes as basic hydraulic pistons.
